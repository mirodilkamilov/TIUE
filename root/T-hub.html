English language programs

Multi Level English

The three-level English language program (ELP) helps those who want to start learning English from scratch or strengthen the basic knowledge of students and move on to the next stage of improving their language skills using the international English language testing system (IELTS).
This course is designed so that each new level is based on the skills you acquired at the previous level. Below you can find out what knowledge and skills you will gain after completing the course and why our courses are more effective.
Each level consists of a twelve-week course that lasts 3 months. Classes are held 6 hours a week, 4.5 of which are contact.

English Language Program Levels

Starter Level (Elementary)
Starter Level (elementary) is an integrated skills course in which each part is divided into grammar, vocabulary, work skills, and everyday English.

Course requirements: entrance test score corresponding to level A1.

The course aims to achieve the following goals:

1. Understanding simple texts and a wide range of common vocabulary in context;
2. Describe aspects of everyday life both orally and in writing;
3. Create short and simple connected texts on familiar topics.
The initial level of the program aims to provide students with a basic understanding of the language, using their basic skills.
Mover Level(Pre-Intermediate)
It is aimed at developing and further improving the level of language proficiency to intermediate (intermediate) - B1 in the pan-European competences of foreign language proficiency.

At this level, students build confidence and lay the Foundation for future success in English.

Course requirements: entrance test score corresponding to level A2.

The course aims to help students achieve a General level of English proficiency. Successful graduates of this course become independent users of the language, which is defined as level B1 in the common European foreign language proficiency (CEFR), developing:
● conversational skills;
● expressing ideas and thoughts;
ability to cope with problems and situations in which they encounter unfamiliar material.
Flyer Level (medium level)
It aims to develop and further improve the level of language proficiency to B2 level in the pan-European foreign language proficiency competencies. Level B2 corresponds to a more advanced, more independent level than the previous levels. Students who have reached this level can communicate easily and without special training, Express their thoughts clearly and in detail.

Course requirements: entrance test score corresponding to level B1 or higher.

At the end of the three-level course, you will be able to:
Understand and extract important information from a written or spoken text on a familiar topic;
● Skim through short text to get specific information;
● Predict the meaning of unfamiliar vocabulary using contextual hints;
● Control a set of individual words and phrases related to specific everyday topics, such as Hobbies, shopping, food and nutrition, weather and seasons, household goods, urban and rural life, etc . ;
● Perform various social functions, including greeting, meeting and saying goodbye, sending requests, suggestions, invitations and apologies and responding to them, performing simple operations in stores and offices, requesting and indicating directions, etc.;
● Describe people, places, likes, dislikes and daily routines in a series of simple phrases and sentences;
● Write short and simple paragraphs describing people, places, and events;
● Correctly formulate the first and main sentence in the paragraph, which is the key to success in writing an essay;
● Use linking words and relative pronouns to effectively organize sentences in a paragraph;
● To understand the form and function of basic tenses including the present simple and continuous, past simple and going to future;
● Use a number of auxiliary verbs (am / is / are / was / were / can / could), as well as correct and irregular verbs;
Understand basic grammatical features and functions, including interrogative words, comparative and superlatives, goal infinitives, calculable and uncountable nouns, determinants, possessive cases, and place prepositions.

The program combines all skills (reading, writing, speaking, and listening), with grammar taught in context and vocabulary notebooks used to facilitate learning.

Course structure
ELP is a set of programs that develop English language skills from elementary to intermediate level in 3 stages. The program helps students prepare for further study in English.

ELP starts at the entry level, Starter Level, and progresses to the intermediate level of English proficiency at the Flyer Level.

Students can enter a particular level at any stage, depending on the results of the entrance tests.

Each stage (12 weeks) consists of 72 hours of training, of which-contact, including intermediate and final tests at the end of the level, which are considered as practical exercises. After passing the tests, students receive feedback from teachers with recommendations.

Classes are held three times a week and last 1.5 astronomical hours.

In addition, the course requires a minimum of 96 hours of self-education on the part of students at each stage, which guarantees the success of students.

After completing the Flyer level, students are ready to start studying academic English in the IELTS (international English language assessment system) format.

Attention: English language Learning in IELTS format is recommended for students over 16 years of age.

About the course

IELTS (International English Language Testing System) is the world's most popular English language proficiency test.
Students starting this course must have a good knowledge of basic English, which is useful when taking the IELTS exams. While studying the IELTS test system, students also develop General English skills.
IELTS preparation courses use a free and informal approach to choosing the curriculum for each level consisting of four modules, as well as when choosing the methods of teaching and learning the language used in the classroom.

We offer training in three stages:

Level 1 (IELTS 5.0 - 5.5)
The first level of the three-level IELTS program is intended for students who are at least B1 level and want to score 5 or 5.5 points in IELTS.

After successful completion of IELTS Level 1 students will be able to:
Use a range of simple and appropriate words, phrases, and expressions to convey your thoughts;
● Freely track the meaning of familiar topics discussed in everyday life;
● Take part in discussions on familiar topics and topics of the immediate environment at any stage of the discussion;
● Understand short simple texts on familiar topics and topics from the immediate environment;
● Create a three-paragraph context on familiar topics and topics in the immediate environment;
● Provide reasons and explanations in oral and written form.

Course requirements: entrance test score corresponding to level B1 or higher.
IELTS Level 2 (IELTS 6.0 - 6.5)
The second level of the three-level IELTS program is designed for students who are at least B2 and want to get an IELTS Band score of 6 or 6.5.

After successful completion of the IELTS Level 2 course, students will be able to:
● Use the appropriate vocabulary and language for a number of specific tasks:
    Develop and support the main arguments, both orally and in writing
    Explain points of view or perspectives both orally and in writing
● Read and understand the main meaning of texts on a number of topics, both familiar and unfamiliar Make up three or four paragraphs on a number of topics.

Course requirements: entrance test score corresponding to level B2 or higher.
IELTS Level 3 (IELTS 7.0 - 7.5+)
This course is designed for students who are at least B2-C1 and want to get an IELTS Band score of 7 or 7.5.

After successful completion of the IELTS Level 3 course, students will be able to:
● Use the appropriate language and vocabulary for specific tasks and goals
● Support and justify opinions and personal views, both orally and in writing
    Understand the main ideas and flow of ideas in complex texts on both familiar and unfamiliar topics
    Develop arguments by evaluating alternatives and counterarguments
● To understand and follow oral and written texts on a variety of familiar and unfamiliar topics. Write three to five paragraphs on a wide range of topics.

Course requirements: entrance test score corresponding to level B2 - C1 and higher.

The programs combine all skills (reading, writing, speaking, and listening), with grammar taught in context, and vocabulary notebooks used to facilitate learning.
